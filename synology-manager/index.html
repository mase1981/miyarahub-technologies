How It Works

Direct API Connection. No Cloud Required.

Syno Manager communicates directly with your Synology NAS using the official DSM API. Your NAS data stays between your device and your NAS.

1

Add Your NAS

Enter your NAS IP address and port. Syno Manager supports local network connections and custom ports - connect to any accessible DSM instance.

2

Authenticate with DSM

Log in with your existing DSM credentials. The app uses the standard Synology authentication API - the same one DSM uses in your browser.

3

Monitor & Manage

Get real-time data streamed directly from your NAS. CPU usage, Docker containers, camera feeds, storage health - all updated live at your chosen polling interval.

Security & Privacy

Your NAS, Your Data, Your Control

Syno Manager is designed with a privacy-first architecture. Minimal data collection, no user tracking, no cloud dependency.

🔒

No Cloud Relay

All communication happens directly between your device and your NAS over your local network. Nothing is routed through external servers.

🔑

Standard DSM Authentication

Uses the same authentication mechanism as the DSM web interface. Your credentials are sent only to your NAS - never to us.

📡

Minimal Analytics

We use Firebase Crashlytics to catch bugs and improve stability - no personal data, no usage tracking, no ads. The app never collects NAS data or credentials beyond your device.

👤

DSM Permission Aware

The app respects DSM user permissions. It can only access what your DSM account has permission to see - no privilege escalation, no admin shortcuts.

🛡️

No Account Required

No sign-up, no email, no MiyaraHub account needed. Download the app, add your NAS, and you're done.

📦

Read-Only by Default

Most features are read-only monitoring. Management actions (Docker stop/restart) require explicit user interaction - nothing happens automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Syno Manager.

"Synology" and "DiskStation Manager" (DSM) are registered trademarks of Synology Inc. Our use of these names is purely for descriptive purposes to indicate compatibility (nominative fair use).
How does the app connect to my NAS? ▼
Syno Manager uses the documented Synology DSM API endpoints to communicate with your NAS over your local network. The app sends requests directly to your NAS - there are no intermediary servers, proxies, or cloud relays involved. You provide the NAS IP/hostname and port, authenticate with your DSM credentials, and the app communicates directly with DSM.
What permissions does the app need on my NAS? ▼
The app operates within whatever permissions your DSM user account has. For full dashboard functionality, an administrator account is recommended. However, the app will gracefully degrade - if your account doesn't have Docker permissions, the Docker section simply won't show data. Same for Surveillance Station, shared folders, etc. No special DSM packages need to be installed.
Does the app store my NAS credentials? ▼
Connection details are stored locally on your device using platform-secure storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore). Credentials are never transmitted to MiyaraHub Technologies or any third party. They are used exclusively to authenticate with your NAS.
Which Synology models are supported? ▼
Syno Manager works with any Synology NAS running DSM 7.0 or later. This includes all current DiskStation (DS), RackStation (RS), and FlashStation (FS) models. Older models running DSM 6.x may have limited functionality due to API differences.
Can I manage Docker containers remotely? ▼
Yes. You can view container status, CPU/memory usage, access logs, and perform stop/restart operations directly from the app. Container creation and configuration changes are not currently supported - the focus is on monitoring and quick management actions.
Is the app free? ▼
Pricing will be announced closer to launch. Our goal is to keep it accessible while sustaining ongoing development and support.
Can I access my NAS remotely (outside my home network)? ▼
Syno Manager connects to whatever IP/hostname you configure. If your NAS is accessible via VPN, Tailscale, or a forwarded port, the app will work remotely. We recommend using a VPN or Tailscale rather than exposing DSM directly to the internet.
