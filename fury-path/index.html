How It Works

Direct Wi-Fi Connection. No Cloud Required.

FuryPath communicates directly with your HDFury device over HTTP on your local network. Your device data never touches an external server.

1

Add Your Device

Enter the IP address of your HDFury device and select the model. FuryPath auto-detects the device on connection and configures the correct TCP port and feature set for your model.

2

Connect Over Wi-Fi

FuryPath communicates via HTTP GET requests directly to your HDFury device on your local network, using the same protocol HDFury uses for its built-in web interface.

3

Control Everything

Monitor signal info in real time, manage EDID profiles, control HDR and Dolby Vision injection, configure CEC/eARC, set up JVC Macros, and tweak every device setting, all from your phone.

Security & Privacy

Local Only. No Account. No Cloud.

FuryPath is designed with a privacy-first, local-only architecture. No cloud dependency, no user account, no data collection beyond crash reporting.

🔒

No Cloud Relay

All commands and status polling happen directly between your phone and your HDFury device over your local Wi-Fi. Nothing leaves your network.

👤

No Account Required

No sign-up, no email, no MiyaraHub account needed. Open the app, add your device IP, and you're in control.

📡

Minimal Analytics

We use Firebase Crashlytics to catch bugs and improve stability. No personal data, no usage tracking, no ads. Your device settings and HDFury data never leave your phone.

💾

Local Storage Only

Device IP addresses and app preferences are stored locally on your device using SharedPreferences. Nothing is synced to a server.

🛡️

Read + Write Control

The app sends commands directly to your HDFury device on your explicit action. Nothing happens automatically or in the background without your knowledge.

📶

Network Permission Only

The only device permission FuryPath requires is internet/network access, used exclusively to connect to your HDFury device on your local Wi-Fi network.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about FuryPath.

"HDFury," "VRRooM," "VERTEX2," and all related product names are trademarks of their respective owners. Our use of these names is purely to describe compatibility.
How does FuryPath communicate with my HDFury device? ▼
FuryPath sends HTTP GET requests directly to your HDFury device on your local Wi-Fi network, using the same protocol used by HDFury's built-in web interface. Commands go to GET /cmd?key=value and status polling reads from GET /ssi/*.ssi endpoints. No external servers are involved. Your device must be on the same Wi-Fi network as your phone.
Which Android version is required? ▼
FuryPath requires Android 5.0 (API 21) or higher. It is an Android-only app for now. iOS support may be added in a future release.
Can I control my HDFury device remotely from outside my home? ▼
FuryPath connects to whatever IP address you configure. If your HDFury device is accessible via VPN or Tailscale, the app will work remotely. We recommend using a VPN or Tailscale rather than exposing your device directly to the internet.
Does it support all HDFury models? ▼
FuryPath supports VRRooM, VERTEX2, VERTEX, DIVA, Maestro, ARCANA2, Arcana, and Dr.HDMI 8K. The app auto-detects your device on connection and activates the feature set relevant to your model. Not all models support all features. JVC Macros, for example, are VRRooM-specific.
How much does FuryPath cost? ▼
FuryPath is a one-time purchase at $4.99. No subscription, no ads, no in-app purchases. You buy it once and own it. Pricing may change before launch.
Can I manage multiple HDFury devices? ▼
Yes. FuryPath supports multiple devices, each with its own settings, favorite tabs, and connection state. Switch between devices from the main dashboard with a single tap.
What is the Favorite Tabs feature? ▼
Each device has 8 feature tabs (INFO, EDID, HDR/AVI, DV, CEC/eARC, MACROS, OSD, CONFIG). You can pin up to 5 of them to the bottom navigation bar per device, so your most-used controls are always one tap away. Each device remembers its own favorites independently.
